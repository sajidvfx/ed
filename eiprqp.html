Part A

1. What is value proposition?

A value proposition is a statement that explains the benefits of a product or service and why customers should choose it over other options. It's the reason a customer would buy a product or service. It communicates the specific value that a product provides to customers and focuses on the overall value that a product or service delivers.

2. Explain how to explain value prop

To determine and explain a value proposition, you should:

  • Identify the product or service's benefits
  • Communicate the value of those benefits
  • Identify a problem that a consumer might face
  • Relate the problem to the value that the product or service provides
  • Ensure relevancy (how well it aligns with customer needs)
  • Highlight the benefit (the positive outcome for the customer)

3. What is business plan framework - Components

A business plan framework includes these key components:

  • Summary: A high-level summary of the business plan
  • Company description: Includes the company's value proposition
  • Products and services: Description of offerings
  • Market analysis: Analysis of market and competitors
  • Marketing plan: Strategy for marketing products/services
  • Management and organization: Description of business structure
  • Financial plan: Financial projections and requirements
  • Risk, return, and exit: Plan for risk management and exit strategy

4. Core functions of prototype development advisor

The core functions of a prototype development advisor include:

  1. Overseeing Prototype Design - Guiding teams through design processes for new products
  2. Prototyping and Material Selection - Selecting appropriate materials and techniques
  3. Testing and Evaluation - Coordinating testing sessions and gathering feedback
  4. Quality Assurance and Compliance - Ensuring prototypes meet regulatory requirements and standards

5. Customer profiling & why it is important in market segmentation

Customer profiling is a marketing strategy that involves gathering and analyzing customer data to create a detailed picture of a company's ideal customer. It includes analyzing demographics, psychographics, behaviors, and geographical location.

Importance in market segmentation:

  • Helps tailor strategies to specific customer groups
  • Identifies better-fit prospects, reducing customer acquisition costs
  • Reduces customer churn and increases loyalty
  • Improves customer service through personalization
  • Guides product decisions based on customer needs
  • Enables more targeted marketing and communications

6. Major steps involved in prototype development

The major steps involved in prototype development are:

  1. Generating Concepts - Ideation and evaluation of potential concepts
  2. Research and Analysis - Understanding target audience and market needs
  3. Detailed Blueprinting - Creating technical specifications and design plans
  4. Preliminary Model Creation - Building initial prototypes for testing
  5. Beta Release and Refinement Cycle - Testing with users and gathering feedback
  6. Product Launch - Finalizing the product for market release

7. Partners involved in prototype development

Partners involved in prototype development include:

  • Manufacturers: Can make adjustments to existing products and provide samples
  • Big design firms: Have teams of engineers and designers for the entire product development process
  • Product development companies: Specialized in prototype creation
  • Machine shops: Help with physical prototype fabrication
  • Engineering firms: Provide technical expertise for prototype development

9. Investors & its types

Investors are individuals or entities who commit capital with the expectation of receiving financial returns. Major types include:

1. Angel Investors

High-net-worth private individuals who provide financial capital to startups or entrepreneurs, typically in exchange for equity. They often invest in early stages when risk is high, using excess cash for these high-risk investments.

2. Venture Capitalists

Private equity investors, usually companies, that invest in startups and small businesses with growth potential. Unlike angel investors, they typically target businesses already in early stages rather than just starting out. They take an equity stake, help nurture growth, and then sell their stake for profit.

3. P2P Lending

Peer-to-peer lending where financing comes directly from individuals, bypassing traditional intermediaries like banks. Examples include crowdfunding platforms where businesses raise capital from many investors online.

4. Personal Investors

Individuals investing their own capital in stocks, bonds, mutual funds, and ETFs. These are not professional investors but people seeking higher returns than basic investment vehicles like certificates of deposit.

5. Institutional Investors

Organizations that invest on behalf of others, such as mutual funds, ETFs, hedge funds, and pension funds. They raise large amounts of capital from many investors, allowing them to purchase substantial assets and potentially influence asset prices.

10. Strategic planning model (Issue-based)

The Issue-based strategic planning model (also called goal-based planning model) is a comprehensive approach for creating a more detailed strategic plan. It's suitable for organizations with basic strategic planning experience that need a more comprehensive plan.

Key steps in the Issue-based model:

  1. Conduct a SWOT analysis: Assess the organization's strengths, weaknesses, opportunities, and threats to identify what the strategic plan should focus on.
  2. Identify and prioritize major issues or goals: Based on the SWOT analysis, determine the highest priority issues and goals that need addressing.
  3. Develop main strategies: Create overarching strategies to address the highest-priority goals and issues, aiming to develop one strategy for the top priority.
  4. Update or create mission and vision statements: Ensure the organization's statements align with the new or updated strategy.
  5. Create action plans: Develop plans to address the goals, including resource needs, roles, and responsibilities.
  6. Develop a yearly operational plan: Create a document to capture goals, progress, and opportunities for improvement in the next cycle.
  7. Allocate resources for the year-one operational plan.
  8. Monitor and revise the strategic plan: Track progress and record lessons learned for future planning cycles.
